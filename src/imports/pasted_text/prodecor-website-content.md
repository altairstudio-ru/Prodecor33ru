Create a premium website for an interior design and turnkey renovation studio: prodecor33.ru.

Business context:
The company works in Vladimir and Vladimir region, creating premium interior design, turnkey renovation, project support, and furnishing solutions for apartments, houses, and commercial spaces.

Goal of the website:
Build trust, show premium quality, communicate expertise, and convert visitors into requests for consultation or cost estimation. The website should feel calm, luxurious, architectural, and reliable.

Audience:
Affluent clients who value aesthetics, quality, comfort, predictability, transparency, and a polished premium experience.

Visual style:
- Warm minimalism.
- Premium interior brand.
- Clean, calm, elegant, and spacious.
- More like an architectural studio or interior magazine than a construction company.
- Lots of air, large headlines, subtle lines, refined spacing, soft shadows, and balanced composition.
- Desktop-first design with responsive adaptation for tablet and mobile.

Color palette:
- Main background: #F5F3EF.
- Main text: #1F1F1F.
- Secondary text: #8A8A8A.
- Accent: #C6A96B.
- Additional neutrals: #2A2A2A, #D1D1D1, #E6E6E6, #FFFFFF.
- Use the gold accent only for CTA buttons, key markers, active states, and important highlights.

Typography:
- Headings: Playfair Display.
- Body text: Inter.
- Optional fallback UI font: Montserrat.
- Headings should be large, elegant, and editorial.
- Body text should be clean, readable, and modern.

Image direction:
- Use premium real-photography style visuals only.
- Do not use generic stock-looking images.
- Hero section: show a large high-end interior with warm natural daylight, neutral materials, wood, stone, brass, clean architectural composition, no people, no clutter, no stock-photo feeling.
- Portfolio blocks: use editorial-style project images with different angles and crops, emphasizing atmosphere, space, materials, and detail.
- Moodboard or support sections: use close-up material shots such as stone, wood, fabric, metal, warm beige, taupe, and graphite surfaces.
- Keep all visuals coherent, minimal, and luxurious.
- Do not use illustrations instead of photos.
- Do not use random domestic scenes.
- Do not make images too bright or empty.
- Do not mix too many visual styles in one page.

Text content direction:
Create realistic Russian-language marketing copy for a premium interior design and renovation studio in Vladimir / Vladimir region. The tone should be confident, calm, premium, and trustworthy. Avoid generic template phrases and avoid overly aggressive sales language.

Main message:
The company creates premium interior design and turnkey renovation projects for apartments, houses, and commercial spaces. The key values are aesthetics, quality, transparency, comfort, and predictable results.

For the hero section, generate:
- a strong H1 about premium interior design and turnkey renovation;
- a short subtitle about creating thoughtful, stylish, and functional interiors;
- 3 short benefit points about experience, premium quality, and full project support;
- 1 primary CTA related to consultation or cost estimate;
- 1 secondary CTA related to portfolio or project discussion.

For the services section, use clear service labels and short descriptions such as:
- Interior design.
- Turnkey renovation.
- Project support and author supervision.
- Material selection and furnishing.

For the portfolio section, add short case-style captions in the format:
- task;
- solution;
- result.
Make the text sound like real completed projects, not generic promotional copy.

For the trust section, include:
- experience and completed projects;
- working under contract;
- transparent estimates;
- deadlines and quality control;
- personalized approach.

For the process section, describe 4–5 clear steps:
1. Application.
2. Measurement and briefing.
3. Concept and estimate.
4. Implementation.
5. Delivery.

For the quiz or form section, use a simple lead-magnet style message:
- receive an initial consultation;
- get a preliminary estimate;
- get recommendations for your project.

For the FAQ section, generate short practical questions and answers about:
- price;
- deadlines;
- what is included;
- how the work process is organized;
- what types of objects are accepted.

For the final CTA, use a stronger closing message that encourages the user to leave a request and discuss the project.

Website structure:
1. Header with logo, menu, phone, and CTA button.
2. Hero screen with strong headline, subtitle, 3 benefits, 2 buttons, and a large premium interior image.
3. Services block with 3–4 cards.
4. Portfolio / cases block with visually strong project cards.
5. “How we work” block with 4–5 steps.
6. Trust and benefits block with numbers, guarantees, deadlines, and transparency.
7. Quiz or mini-form block.
8. Testimonials block.
9. FAQ accordion.
10. Final CTA block on a dark background.
11. Footer with contacts, social links, and extra navigation.

Layout and UX:
- Use a clear grid and strong hierarchy.
- Keep the page premium, calm, and easy to scan.
- Avoid visual overload.
- Make the primary CTA stand out clearly.
- Use light cards with thin borders and subtle shadows.
- Keep forms simple with rounded corners and comfortable spacing.

Button style:
- Primary button: fill #C6A96B with white text.
- Secondary button: light outline style.
- Hover states should be soft and premium.

Copy rules:
- Keep the text concise, premium, and easy to scan.
- Use natural Russian, not translation-like phrasing.
- Avoid clichés like “individual approach” repeated too often.
- Avoid aggressive sales wording.
- Keep headings short and elegant.
- Make the text believable for a real interior studio website.

Negative constraints:
- No acidic colors.
- No heavy shadows.
- No noisy gradients.
- No aggressive CTA styling.
- No generic construction-company look.
- No visual clutter.
- No cheap luxury effect.
- No random stock imagery.
- No excessive gold everywhere.

Final outcome:
The website should feel like a premium interior studio with a strong hero section, clean structure, calm and confident presentation, editorial aesthetics, and high conversion potential.
